Don't "Longhorn" yourself on projects.

Software development has taught me many things, and one of them is to always have a consistent feature flow on updates. Picture this, you are working on a big update, and while trying to pack as many new features in you realize it's hard to backtrack. Theres many untested, broken, half complete, buggy, and unnessesary features you started to create and now you can't go back and fix all of it because there's a deadline soon. There are some good finished features in the mix, but there's more broken features in the mix. The only way out would be to completely reset development and to "clear your mind" in a sense, and take it slowly and release these in later updates. You don't want to do this. It cuts out a ton of potential. TBD
